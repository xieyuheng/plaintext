Ishikawa diagram in plain text

In case you didn't know, the Ishikawa, or fishbone diagram
is a tool for exploring root cause of a problem with a team of people.
(see the link below.)

The six main types of failure mode
can be represented on each leg
and thought about in relation to the problem,
represented by the head of the 'fish'.

The beauty of this though,
when the team have all had their say
and it's time to record the findings and share,
this is instant.
You do not have to keep those oversized A2 sheets covered in scribbles.

Also, the following step is sometimes the Fmea tool
and this can be related directly to each failure mode
via a notation system for the fmea matrix.
